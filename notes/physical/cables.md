# Network Cables

## Types of Network Cables

### 1. Copper Cables

Copper cables transmit data as electrical signals and are commonly used for short to medium distances.

#### Twisted Pair Cables

- **Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP)**: The most common type of networking cable, used in most Ethernet networks.
- **Shielded Twisted Pair (STP)**: Includes additional shielding to reduce electromagnetic interference.

| Category | Max Speed    | Max Distance | Common Use              |
|----------|-------------|-------------|-------------------------|
| CAT-5    | 100 Mbps    | 100 m       | Legacy networks         |
| CAT-5e   | 1 Gbps      | 100 m       | Home and office networks|
| CAT-6    | 10 Gbps     | 55 m        | High-speed networks     |
| CAT-6a   | 10 Gbps     | 100 m       | Data centers            |
| CAT-7    | 10 Gbps     | 100 m       | Enterprise networks     |

#### Coaxial Cables

- Used historically for Ethernet and still used for cable television and broadband internet.
- Consists of a central conductor, insulation, metallic shield, and outer jacket.

### 2. Fiber Optic Cables

Fiber optic cables transmit data as pulses of light, offering much higher speeds and longer distances than copper cables.

- **Single-Mode Fiber (SMF)**: Uses a single light path, suitable for long-distance transmission (up to 100 km).
- **Multi-Mode Fiber (MMF)**: Uses multiple light paths, suitable for shorter distances (up to 2 km).

| Feature         | Copper Cable           | Fiber Optic Cable        |
|-----------------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Speed           | Up to 10 Gbps         | Up to 400 Gbps          |
| Distance        | Up to 100 m           | Up to 100 km            |
| Interference    | Susceptible to EMI     | Immune to EMI            |
| Cost            | Lower                  | Higher                   |
| Installation    | Easier                 | More complex             |

## Connector Types

- **RJ-45**: Standard connector for Ethernet cables (twisted pair).
- **SC, LC, ST**: Common fiber optic connectors.
- **BNC**: Used with coaxial cables.

## Choosing the Right Cable

- **Short distances and cost-sensitive**: Use copper twisted pair (CAT-5e or CAT-6).
- **High speed and long distances**: Use fiber optic cables.
- **Environments with high interference**: Use shielded twisted pair (STP) or fiber optic cables.
